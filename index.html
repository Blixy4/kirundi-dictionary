Abstract

A collaborative lexicographic initiative to document, translate, and digitise the English–Kirundi language pair — making bilingual knowledge freely and permanently accessible to all Burundians.

The English–Kirundi Dictionary project arose from a documented gap in available linguistic resources for Burundi's population. Despite Kirundi being the native language of the overwhelming majority of Burundians, no comprehensive, digitally accessible bilingual dictionary existed to bridge it with English — the primary language of international education, commerce, and scholarship.

This project was conceived and executed by five contributors united by a shared conviction: that access to language is access to opportunity, and that Kirundi deserves the same digital presence afforded to the world's most widely spoken languages.

The idea came when I noticed that many people in my community had problems translating between English and Kirundi. I was inspired by this communication gap, which made me understand the importance of having reliable language resources.

— Bigirimana Stany, English Facilitator
Mission & Goals
  1. I.
    Preserve and elevate Kirundi To produce a rigorous, accurate bilingual lexicon that honours the linguistic structures, idiomatic richness, and cultural context of Kirundi as spoken in Burundi today.
  2. II.
    Expand educational access To equip students, teachers, and educational institutions across Burundi with a reliable English reference tool that functions entirely offline, removing dependency on internet infrastructure.
  3. III.
    Support government and civic institutions To provide public sector workers, policy makers, and civil servants with a trusted translation resource that strengthens official communication and reduces language barriers in governance.
  4. IV.
    Serve the Burundian diaspora To remain a living connection to mother tongue and culture for Burundians studying or working abroad, particularly within the East African academic and refugee communities.
  5. V.
    Establish a foundation for future scholarship To create an open, extensible linguistic dataset that future researchers, lexicographers, and developers may build upon as Kirundi's digital presence continues to grow.
Methodology

Translations were produced collaboratively, with each contributor drawing on native Kirundi fluency alongside formal education in linguistics, translation, psychology, and English language facilitation. Entries were reviewed for contextual accuracy, cultural appropriateness, and consistency of register before inclusion in the final corpus.

The project adopted a community-first review model, prioritising clarity for non-specialist users — students and general readers — while maintaining terminological rigour sufficient for academic and professional use.

Statement of Need

Kirundi is spoken by over 10 million people, yet digital bilingual resources connecting it to English remain critically scarce. This project addresses that gap directly, placing a scholarly tool in the hands of those who need it most.

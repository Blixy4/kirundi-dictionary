Abstract

A collaborative lexicographic initiative to document, translate, and digitise the English–Kirundi language pair — making bilingual knowledge freely and permanently accessible to all Burundians and every Kirundi speaker around the world.

The English–Kirundi Dictionary project arose from a documented gap in available linguistic resources for the Burundian population. Despite Kirundi being the native language of the overwhelming majority of Burundians, no comprehensive, digitally accessible bilingual dictionary existed to bridge it with English — the primary language of international education, commerce, and scholarship.

The project was conceived and executed by a team of contributors united by a shared conviction: that access to language is access to opportunity, and that Kirundi deserves the same digital presence afforded to the world's most widely spoken languages.

Building this dictionary meant noticing a gap in the community and choosing to fill it — together, patiently, and in service of every Burundian who would one day open it.

— Bigirimana Stany, Initial Spark of the Project
Mission & Goals
  1. I.
    Preserve and elevate Kirundi To produce a rigorous, accurate bilingual lexicon that honours the linguistic structures, idiomatic richness, and cultural context of Kirundi as spoken in Burundi today.
  2. II.
    Expand educational access To equip students, teachers, and educational institutions across Burundi with a reliable English reference tool that functions entirely offline, removing dependency on internet infrastructure.
  3. III.
    Support government and civic institutions To provide public sector workers, policy makers, and civil servants with a trusted translation resource that strengthens official communication and reduces language barriers in governance.
  4. IV.
    Serve the global Burundian community To remain a living connection to mother tongue and culture for Burundians studying, working, or living abroad — keeping Kirundi present wherever Burundians are present.
  5. V.
    Establish a foundation for future scholarship To create an open, extensible linguistic dataset that future researchers, lexicographers, and developers may build upon as Kirundi's digital presence continues to grow.
Methodology

Translations were produced collaboratively, with each contributor drawing on native Kirundi fluency alongside formal education in linguistics, translation, psychology, and English language facilitation. Entries were reviewed for contextual accuracy, cultural appropriateness, and consistency of register before inclusion in the final corpus.

The Kirundi orthography used throughout the application follows the rules and guidelines published by Ikirundi.org, ensuring linguistic correctness consistent with national standards. The work was further reviewed by Dr. Mberamihigo of the Université du Burundi, and 83% of the Kirundi vocabulary has been formally certified by the Académie Rundi — the official national authority for the language.

Statement of Need

Kirundi is spoken by over 10 million people, yet digital bilingual resources connecting it to English remain critically scarce. This project addresses that gap directly — placing a scholarly tool in the hands of those who need it most.
